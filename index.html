Conor McGregor

The Notorious

Lets have a little look into the life of Ireland's much loved mixed martial artist:

  • 14 July 1988 - Conor Anthony McGregor was born in Crumlin, Dublin.
  • 17 February 2007 - At the age of 18, Conor made his amatuer mixed martial arts debut against Kieran Campbell and won with a first round TKO.
  • 9 March 2008 - McGregor made his proffesional debut against Gary Morris, securing another TKO, this time in the second round.
  • 2 June 2012 - Conor won the Cage Warriors Featherweight Championship after a second round submission (via RNC) of Dave Hill in Dublin.
  • 31 December 2012 - McGregor won the Cage Warriors Lightweight Championship to become the first European Professional Mixed Martial Artist to hold titles in two divisions simultaniously.
  • 6 April 2013 - Conor returned to featherweight in his UFC debut against the battle hardened Marcus Brimage, taking the victory with a first round TKO and also earning Knockout Of The Night.
  • 11 July 2015 - Just as expected, Conor smashed his way through the UFC roster and went on to challenge Chad Mendes for the UFC Interim Featherweight Championship. McGregor got the win in the second round via TKO, securing a match up against Jose Aldo.
  • 12 December 2015 - Conor goes on to fight Jose Aldo for the unified UFC Featherweight Championship, knocking him out in 13 seconds.
  • 5 March 2016 - McGregor suffers his first loss inside the octagon against Stockton, California's Nate Diaz. Diaz gets the win via second round submission (rear naked choke).
  • 20 August 2016 - The rematch between Diaz and Mcgregor. The battle lasted the full 25 minutes, with Conor taking the win by majority decision.
  • 12 November 2016 - Conor goes on to fight Eddie Alvarez, in the lightweight division for the UFC Lightweight Championship. Conor wins the fight in the second round via TKO, but was then stripped of the title due to inactivity.
  • 6 October 2018 - Nearly two years later, Conor re-emerges in the octagon to fight Khabib Nurmagomedov for the UFC Lightweight Title, in one of the UFC's most anticipated match ups. Khabib goes on to win the fight via a neck crank in Round 4.
